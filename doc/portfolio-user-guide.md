# Public Portfolio - User Guide

## 🎯 What is a Public Portfolio?

Your public portfolio is a beautiful, shareable webpage that showcases your projects to recruiters, clients, and colleagues. It's like having your own professional website, powered by the projects you already track in DevTrack.

---

## 🚀 Getting Started

### Step 1: Create Your Profile

1. **Log in** to your DevTrack account
2. Click on **Profile** in the sidebar
3. Fill in your profile information:
   - **Username** (required): Choose a unique username (e.g., `john-doe`)
     - 3-30 characters
     - Lowercase letters, numbers, hyphens, and underscores only
     - This will be part of your portfolio URL
   - **Display Name** (required): Your full name or professional name
   - **Bio**: Tell people about yourself (up to 1000 characters)
   - **Location**: Where you're based (optional)

4. Click **Save Changes**

### Step 2: Add Social Links

In the **Social Links** section:

1. Add your social media URLs:
   - GitHub
   - LinkedIn
   - Twitter
   - Personal Website

2. Use the **Show** checkbox next to each link to control visibility

3. Click **Save Changes**

### Step 3: Choose Your Projects

In the **Portfolio Projects** section:

1. See your available projects listed
2. Click **Add** next to any project you want to showcase
3. Click **Remove** to take a project off your portfolio
4. Added projects appear in the order you add them

💡 **Tip**: Only add your best projects! Quality over quantity.

### Step 4: Configure Privacy

In the **Visibility Settings** section:

1. **Make portfolio public**: Toggle ON to make your portfolio visible to everyone
   - ⚠️ Your portfolio is **private** by default
   
2. **Available for work**: Toggle ON to show a "💼 Available for Work" badge
   
3. **Show email address**: Toggle ON to display your email on your portfolio

4. Click **Save Changes**

### Step 5: Share Your Portfolio

Once your portfolio is public:

1. Find your **Portfolio URL** section
2. Your link will look like: `https://trackmydevelopement.com/portfolio/your-username`
3. Click **Copy Link** to copy it to your clipboard
4. Share it anywhere:
   - Resume
   - LinkedIn profile
   - Email signature
   - Social media bio

## 📊 Track Your Impact

In the **Analytics** section, you can see:

- **Total Views**: How many times your portfolio has been viewed
- **Last Viewed**: When someone last looked at your portfolio

💡 **Note**: Your own views don't count towards the total!

## 🎨 What Visitors See

When someone visits your portfolio, they'll see:

### Profile Section
- Your profile photo (if added)
- Display name
- Location
- "Available for Work" badge (if enabled)
- Bio
- Social links (GitHub, LinkedIn, Twitter, Website)
- Email (if enabled)

### Projects Section
- Project name and type
- Category and role
- Description
- Responsibilities
- Highlights
- Tech stack tags
- Project dates
- Team size
- Repository link (if enabled)

## 💡 Best Practices

### 1. Craft a Compelling Bio
Your bio is the first thing visitors read. Make it count:
- ✅ Who you are
- ✅ What you do
- ✅ What you're passionate about
- ✅ What you're looking for (if anything)

**Example:**
```
Full-stack developer with 5 years of experience building scalable web applications. 
Passionate about React, TypeScript, and cloud architecture. 
Currently seeking senior engineering roles in fintech.
```

### 2. Choose Your Best Projects
Quality > Quantity. Select projects that:
- ✅ Show off your skills
- ✅ Are relevant to your goals
- ✅ Have clear descriptions
- ✅ Are recent (or impressive older work)

**Tip**: 3-6 projects is usually ideal.

### 3. Write Clear Project Descriptions
For each project, include:
- What the project does
- Your role and responsibilities
- Technologies used
- Impact or results

### 4. Use Professional Photos
If you add a profile photo:
- ✅ Use a clear, professional headshot
- ✅ Good lighting and background
- ❌ Avoid casual selfies or group photos

### 5. Keep It Updated
- Update your bio when your situation changes
- Add new impressive projects
- Remove outdated projects
- Refresh your availability status

### 6. Add Social Proof
Link to:
- Your active GitHub (shows code quality)
- Your professional LinkedIn
- Your technical blog (if you have one)

## 🐛 Troubleshooting

### "Username already taken"
- Try a different username
- Add numbers or hyphens: `john-doe-dev`, `johndoe123`

### "Portfolio not found"
- Make sure **Make portfolio public** is toggled ON
- Wait a minute after saving changes
- Try logging out and accessing the link in a private browser window

### Changes not showing
- Click **Save Changes** button
- Refresh your portfolio preview
- Clear your browser cache

### Projects not appearing
- Make sure you clicked **Add** to add them to portfolio
- Check that your profile is public
- Verify the project has required information filled in
